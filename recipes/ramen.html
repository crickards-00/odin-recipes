Ramen

Raman is a widely popular noodle soup dish throughout Japan with different regions and prefects having unique spins on the beloved dish. The base of any ramen dish is the broth, the 4 main bases used to build up the delicious broth are shoyu (soy sauce) shio (salt), miso (fermented soya-bean paste) and tonkotsu (pork bone broth).

Tonkotsu Ramen

Ingredients

Broth

  • 2.2 kg pork bones
  • 1 large onion, skin on, quartered
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 large peice of ginger, cut into large peices

Ingrediants/Toppings

  • Green onion
  • Soft boil egg
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Ramen Noodles (you can buy fresh or dried, I personally perfer fresh.)

Making the broth

  1. Wash the bones, once done at the a large pot and cover with water, bring to a boil for 10 minutes and skim off the material that floats to the top. (While this is happening roughtly chop the other Ingredients from the broth section)
  2. Strain the bones and discard the cooking water. Rinse and clean the bones.
  3. Add the cleaned bones back to the pot along with the other ingrediants and cover with water. Brings to a boil and let it cook for 10 hours. Keep adding water so it doesnt dry out.
  4. Once the time has been reached, strain the broth.
  5. Bring another pot of water upto a boil and and gentle put the eggs in for 6 minutes
  6. while this is happening wash the veggies and then shop the green onion
  7. Once the 6 minutes are up take the eggs out and put into a bowl of ice water. After eggs have cooled enough to touch, removed the shell,
  8. Bring water back upto a boil and drop in some noodles
  9. Once noodles have cooked to your liking. Remove, add to a bowl, top with broth and garnish with, green onion, bean sprouts and egg. ENJOY!
